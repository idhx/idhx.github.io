I am a cognitive anthropologist by training...

## Education

I completed my Ph.D. in anthropology at the London School of Economics, where I was funded by the ESRC and supervised by Prof Rita Astuti and Prof Charles Stafford. I was then awarded a one-year ESRC postdoctoral fellowship in the Department of Methodology at the LSE, mentored by Dr Eleanor A. Power. I am now an LSE Fellow in the same department.

## Research interests

I study social norms, their origin, and how they change over time. I do this by attending to a combination of social dynamics and evolved aspects of human psychology. More recently, I have also begun thinking of norms themselves as subject to selective pressures and displaying other evolutionary properties. Cultural evolution, as an overarching theoretical stance, offers the most promising way forward for my work.
